Threats to sea turtles

polution caused by plastic

Turtles eat plastic, get caught in it, have to wade through it in the water and on beaches, and microplastic can affect nests and hatchlings.

Entanglement in fish gear

Turtles get caught on long-lines, in shrimp nets, and get injured with bomb fishing.

Global warmings

Rising temperatures increase sand temperatures, which can cause only females to be born. Climate change also impacts sea level rise, which can erode nesting beaches, and cause coral bleaching, an important habitat for hawksbills.
