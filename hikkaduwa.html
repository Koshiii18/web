HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY, SRI LANKA

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, is a conservation center dedicated to protecting and preserving the endangered sea turtles that nest on the island's shores. The hatchery serves as a sanctuary for these majestic creatures, which face numerous threats from human activities and natural predators. The hatchery was established in 1986 by a group of locals who were concerned about the rapidly declining turtle population in Sri Lanka. Over the years, the center has expanded its operations, with a focus on education and awareness-raising as well as conservation efforts. Visitors can tour the facility, learn about the different turtle species found in Sri Lanka, and even participate in releasing baby turtles back into the ocean. One of the key functions of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to protect turtle eggs from predators and poachers. Female sea turtles return to the same beaches where they were born to lay their eggs, but many of these nesting sites have been destroyed or disturbed by human activities such as beachfront development and tourism. Additionally, the eggs are highly prized as a delicacy and aphrodisiac in many Asian countries, leading to illegal poaching and trade. At the hatchery, volunteers collect the eggs and carefully relocate them to protected areas where they can hatch safely. The incubation process takes around 50 to 60 days, depending on the species, and the hatchlings are then released into the ocean. Visitors to the center can witness this amazing process firsthand, as they watch the tiny turtles make their way down the beach and into the water. In addition to protecting turtle eggs, the hatchery also cares for injured or sick turtles that have been rescued from the ocean. Many turtles are injured by fishing nets or other human debris, or suffer from illnesses such as shell rot or infections. The center provides medical treatment and rehabilitation for these turtles, with the goal of releasing them back into the wild once they are healthy enough. Education and awareness-raising are also important components of the hatchery's work. Through guided tours, visitors can learn about the different turtle species found in Sri Lanka, their life cycles, and the threats they face. The center also hosts educational programs for local schools, aimed at teaching children about the importance of conservation and environmental protection. Another way the hatchery promotes conservation is through its turtle adoption program. Visitors can "adopt" a baby turtle for a small fee, which goes towards supporting the center's conservation efforts. Participants receive a certificate with a photo of their adopted turtle, and are invited to release it into the ocean once it has hatched. Overall, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery plays a critical role in protecting and preserving Sri Lanka's sea turtle population. Through its conservation efforts, education and awareness-raising programs, and turtle adoption program, the center is helping to ensure that these magnificent creatures will continue to thrive for generations to come. Visitors to Sri Lanka should not miss the opportunity to visit this incredible facility and witness the beauty and wonder of sea turtles up close.
