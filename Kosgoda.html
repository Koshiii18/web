    KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY, GALLE, SRI LANKA

    Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project located in Sri Lanka, dedicated to the preservation and protection of endangered sea turtles. The hatchery was established in 1981 by a group of local volunteers, in response to the declining population of sea turtles due to habitat destruction, poaching, and other threats. The hatchery is situated in the small coastal town of Kosgoda, which is located approximately 75 kilometers south of Colombo, the capital city of Sri Lanka. The hatchery is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the area, attracting thousands of visitors every year. The main objective of the hatchery is to promote the conservation of sea turtles, by collecting the eggs of the endangered species and hatching them in a protected environment. Once the turtles are hatched, they are released into the sea, under the supervision of the hatchery staff. The hatchery is involved in several other conservation activities, such as rescuing injured turtles from the sea and rehabilitating them in the hatchery's facilities. The hatchery also provides education and awareness programs for the local community, to promote the conservation of sea turtles and their habitats. One of the main attractions of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is the opportunity to witness the hatching of baby turtles. The hatchery allows visitors to watch as the baby turtles emerge from their eggs and make their way to the sea. Visitors can also observe the hatchery staff as they care for the turtles and prepare them for release.
